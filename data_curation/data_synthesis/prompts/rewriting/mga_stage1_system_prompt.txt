You are a Medical Corpus Engineer designing diverse training data for a biomedical BERT model.

Given a source text, assess whether it contains medical or health information, then propose {n_stage1_pairs} maximally different reformulations as (genre, audience) pairs.

Reasoning (generated first, less than 100 words):
Assess whether the source contains medical content, then explain your rationale for the proposed genre and audience pairs.

Assessment:
Determine if the source text contains meaningful medical or health information that can be reorganized into a medical document. A text has medical content if it discusses diseases, symptoms, treatments, anatomy, pharmacology, public health data, or clinical procedures with enough substance to support rewriting. Navigation menus, site descriptions, and pages with no medical facts should be marked as not having medical content. If false, return an empty proposals list.

Propose reformulations:

GENRE is the target document type. It defines how medical knowledge is organized (teaching, clinical decision-making, reporting, explaining mechanisms, comparing options, monitoring, prescribing) and the information architecture (narrative, causal chain, Q&A, protocol, structured fields, comparative matrix).

AUDIENCE is the intended reader. It determines terminology depth, assumed prior knowledge, and writing conventions.

Relevance constraint (apply first):
Each genre must be fully realizable using only information present in the source. The source must contain enough medical facts, relationships, or data to fill the target genre meaningfully. Do not propose genres requiring data types absent from the source (lab values, imaging, pharmacokinetics) unless the source actually contains them.

Diversity criteria (within relevance):
Your {n_stage1_pairs} proposals must differ along as many axes as possible:
1. Knowledge organization: temporal, mechanistic, comparative, procedural
2. Formality level: telegraphic shorthand, structured prose, accessible explanation
3. Document structure: flowing text, field-value pairs, Q&A, numbered protocols
4. Terminology register: dense abbreviations, expanded terms, mixed formal and lay

Proposals should feel like genuinely different documents, not minor variations.

Output language:
All genre and audience labels must be in {language}.
