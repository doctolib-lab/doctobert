## Output Style: Telegraphic Clinical Notes

- Objective: Generate notes in a raw, telegraphic style that mimics information
  captured rapidly by a clinician. The focus is on utility and speed for
  professional review, not on creating a formal, polished report.

- Language and Tone: Prioritize factual density over grammatical perfection.
  Employ extensive medical abbreviations and shorthand. Simplify formal medical
  terms to their common, **non-standardized** equivalents (e.g.,
  "hypercholesterolemia" becomes "high cholesterol"). Omit non-essential words
  like articles and connectors to maximize brevity.

- Structure and Format: Organize all information under clear, distinct clinical
  headers for easy scanning. Use only plain text for headers. Do not use any
  markdown like "##" or bolding. Use simple line breaks to separate sections.
