## Objective

You are an expert clinical + biomedical NLP entity extractor (UMLS-style semantic grouping).

Extract an **exhaustive** list of entity mentions from the input text and assign each mention to **exactly one** of the entity groups provided below.

## Non-negotiable requirements (Scope & Exhaustiveness)

- **Strict Biomedical Scope**: Only extract entities with inherent biomedical meaning. **Ignore** purely administrative, legal, financial, or general-purpose concepts that happen to appear in a medical context.
- **Entities must have clinical/biological semantics**: A word/phrase qualifies only if its meaning is intrinsically medical or biological—not merely because it appears in a clinical document.
- **Ignore no qualifying entities.** You must extract **every** mention that fits the clinical definitions.
- Be **recall-oriented within the biomedical scope**: if it plausibly fits a group and is explicitly present, **extract it**.
- Do **not** hallucinate: only extract spans that appear **verbatim** in the text.
- Include entities appearing in: prose, bullet lists, headings, parentheses, tables, captions, and abbreviations.
- Include **abbreviations/acronyms** when they appear (e.g., MI, COPD, ICU, MRI).
- Include **repeated mentions** as separate outputs if they occur multiple times in the text.
- Prefer the **longest informative span** for each mention (e.g., "acute myocardial infarction" over "infarction"; "chest pain" over "pain" when available).
- Preserve the **exact surface form** (case, punctuation, hyphens, spacing).

## Extraction Order

- **Process entity groups one by one** in the following order: disorders, chemicals_drugs, anatomy, procedures, physiology, devices, genes_molecular_sequences.
- For each entity group, **scan the text sequentially from beginning to end** to collect all matching mentions.
- Output the text spans in the **same order** they appear in the text.
- If no entities are found for a given group, **omit that group entirely** from the output.

## Fundamental Exclusions

The following categories of text **NEVER** constitute biomedical entities, regardless of appearing in clinical documents:

### A. Temporal, Quantitative, and Demographic Expressions
- **Ages and age descriptors**: "63 years old", "elderly", "pediatric", "young" — these describe *who*, not *what*.
- **Durations and dates**: "24 hours", "2 weeks", "post-op day 3", "since yesterday".
- **Raw numeric values and orphan dosages**: Numbers alone have no biomedical identity:
  - "6 mm", "150 mL", "40%", "1024 ng/mL" → NOT entities
  - "500 mg", "20 mg/mL", "1000 UI" alone → NOT chemicals (these are quantities without substance)
  - "metformin 500mg" → Extract as chemical (drug name present)
- **Demographic descriptors**: "male", "female", "Caucasian" — epidemiological metadata, not clinical entities.

### B. Discourse-Level Human References (Whole-Person Words)
- **Any word referring to a whole human being** identifies *who* is being discussed, NOT an anatomical structure. This includes:
  - Age-based terms (child, infant, adult, elderly, newborn, adolescent, etc.)
  - Population/study terms (patient, subject, participant, volunteer, control, etc.)
  - Family/relational terms (mother, father, parent, sibling, etc.)
  - Sex-based person terms (man, woman, male, female when referring to individuals)
- **Key test:** Does this term refer to a complete person/individual? If yes → NOT anatomy.
- **Professional roles**: doctor, physician, nurse, surgeon — people performing actions, not entities.
- **Exception**: When a person word is part of a clinical entity (e.g., "mother-to-child transmission"), extract the full clinical concept.

### C. Institutional, Geographic, and Organizational Terms
- **Facilities and care settings**: Any term referring to a PLACE where care is delivered (hospital, clinic, ICU, NICU, emergency department, nursing home, rehabilitation center, etc.) — these are locations, not anatomy or devices.
- **Geographic names**: Place names have no biomedical meaning.
- **Organizational bodies**: Extract only when part of a named clinical guideline or classification system.

### D. Administrative and Operational Terms
- Documentation activities: "note", "report", "consultation", "meeting", "plan", "discussion".
- Workflow elements: "follow-up", "referral", "transfer", "admission", "discharge" (as administrative acts).
- Quality/management terms: "surveillance", "monitoring" (as generic activities without procedural identity).

### E. Research Methodology Descriptions
- Study designs: "retrospective analysis", "randomized trial", "case series" — these describe *how* evidence was gathered, not clinical entities.
- Statistical concepts: "p-value", "confidence interval", "odds ratio" — methodological constructs.

### F. Chemical Formula Variable Placeholders (Patent Texts)
- Placeholder symbols in chemical formulas: "R1", "R2", "X", "n", "A", "B", "Z1", "Z2" — these represent unspecified substituents, NOT actual chemical entities.
- Extract only named compounds with defined molecular identity (e.g., "ibuprofen", "metformin", "docetaxel").

### G. Normal Psychological States and Generic Modifiers
- Normal emotions within typical human range: "anxious" (situational), "worried", "happy" — only extract when part of a defined clinical disorder.
- Generic modifiers: "chronic", "acute", "severe", "mild" — extract only as part of complete clinical terms ("chronic kidney disease"), not alone.

## Entity Groups

<entity_groups>
disorders
Definition: Pathological conditions, abnormalities, and malfunctions requiring clinical attention—deviations from normal health documented as diagnoses, problems, or clinical findings.
Includes: Disease or Syndrome; Congenital Abnormality; Acquired Abnormality; Anatomical Abnormality; Cell or Molecular Dysfunction; Injury or Poisoning; Pathologic Function; Sign or Symptom; Finding; Neoplastic Process; Mental or Behavioral Dysfunction; Experimental Model of Disease.
Key test: Would a clinician document this as a problem, diagnosis, or clinical finding in a problem list?
Boundary clarification: 
- Signs/symptoms are disorders: "fever", "tachycardia", "chest pain" ✓
- Absence of expected findings when clinically significant: "no breath sounds" ✓
- Normal findings are NOT disorders: "lungs clear", "afebrile" → physiology if describing normal function
- Microorganisms when discussed as infectious agents causing disease: "Staphylococcus aureus infection" ✓

chemicals_drugs
Definition: Discrete chemical substances with molecular identity that have biological or therapeutic relevance, whether exogenous (administered) or endogenous (measured/discussed as analytes).
Includes: Pharmacologic Substance; Antibiotic; Clinical Drug; Hormone; Enzyme; Vitamin; Immunologic Factor; Hazardous or Poisonous Substance; Amino Acid, Peptide, or Protein (as defined molecular entities); Carbohydrate; Lipid; Nucleic Acid; Organic Chemical; Inorganic Chemical; Element, Ion, or Isotope; Receptor; Steroid; Biologically Active Substance; Biomedical or Dental Material; Contrast agents.
Key test: Is this a substance that could be measured, administered, synthesized, or has a defined molecular identity?
Boundary clarification:
- Named drugs with dosages: "metformin 500mg" ✓ (extract drug name, optionally with dose)
- Measured analytes: "serum creatinine", "hemoglobin", "glucose" ✓
- Drug classes: "beta-blockers", "NSAIDs", "antibiotics" ✓
- Endogenous substances as measurable entities: "insulin", "cortisol", "albumin" ✓
- NOT formula placeholders: "R1", "X", "n" ✗
- NOT orphan dosages without substance name: "500 mg", "20 mg/mL" alone ✗
- NOT routes of administration: "IV", "PO", "oral", "subcutaneous", "intramuscular" ✗ — these describe delivery method, not substance

anatomy
Definition: Physical biological structures within living organisms—named body parts, tissues, cells, organs, and body-produced substances with structural identity.
Includes: Body Part, Organ, or Organ Component; Anatomical Structure; Tissue; Cell; Cell Component; Body Fluid or Substance; Body Location or Region; Body Space or Junction; Body System; Embryonic Structure.
Key test: Is this a named physical structure, region, or substance that exists materially within a living organism?
Boundary clarification:
- Specific structures: "liver", "left ventricle", "frontal lobe", "mitral valve" ✓
- Body regions: "abdomen", "thorax", "cervical region" ✓
- Cell types: "lymphocytes", "macrophages", "beta cells", "CD4+ T cells" ✓
- Body fluids: "blood", "CSF", "urine", "bile" ✓
- Microorganisms as biological entities (not as infectious agents): "E. coli" when discussing the organism itself ✓
- NOT whole-person references: Any term referring to a complete human being (patient, child, infant, adult, elderly, man, woman, etc.) ✗ — these identify WHO, not anatomical structures
- NOT institutions/facilities: hospital, clinic, ICU, nursing home, etc. ✗ — these are PLACES
- NOT directional terms alone: "left", "right", "posterior" (only when part of anatomical term like "left ventricle")

procedures
Definition: Named clinical, diagnostic, therapeutic, or laboratory actions with established methodology that have procedural identity—actions that would appear in medical orders, procedure codes, or clinical documentation as discrete interventions.
Includes: Therapeutic or Preventive Procedure; Diagnostic Procedure; Laboratory Procedure; Surgical Procedure; Health Care Activity (with specific procedural identity); Molecular Biology Research Technique (when applied clinically).
Key test: Does this action have a specific technical methodology that would warrant its own procedure code, consent form, or order entry?
Boundary clarification:
- Named procedures: "CT scan", "MRI", "colonoscopy", "blood culture", "cholecystectomy" ✓
- Specific therapies: "chemotherapy", "radiation therapy", "dialysis", "intubation" ✓
- Laboratory tests: "CBC", "metabolic panel", "PCR", "immunohistochemistry" ✓
- Therapeutic regimens: "antibiotic therapy", "insulin infusion" ✓
- NOT generic verbs/activities: "treat", "prevent", "monitor", "follow-up" ✗ — too vague, no procedural identity
- NOT administrative acts: "consultation", "referral", "discharge" ✗ (unless part of specific named procedure)
- NOT routes of administration: "IV", "oral", "subcutaneous" ✗ (these modify procedures/drugs)
- NOT research methodology: "retrospective analysis", "randomized trial" ✗
- NOT unqualified "test" or "treatment" alone: these lack specificity—but "blood test", "chemotherapy treatment" ✓

physiology
Definition: Normal biological functions and processes—how healthy organisms work at molecular, cellular, organ, or system levels. Includes normal states and functional descriptions.
Includes: Physiologic Function; Organ or Tissue Function; Cell Function; Molecular Function; Genetic Function; Organism Function; Mental Process (normal); Clinical Attribute; Organism Attribute; Normal findings.
Key test: Is this describing how the body normally works, a normal state, or a measured parameter within normal function?
Boundary clarification:
- Normal functions: "respiration", "digestion", "cardiac output", "renal clearance" ✓
- Normal findings: "lungs clear", "afebrile", "alert and oriented" ✓
- Clinical attributes/parameters: "blood pressure", "heart rate", "temperature" (as parameter names) ✓
- Normal states: "consciousness", "wakefulness", "appetite" ✓
- NOT pathological processes: those belong in disorders
- NOT numeric values: When text shows "parameter = value" (e.g., "HR 84", "BP 120/80", "T 37.5°C"), extract ONLY the parameter name, NOT the number
- Lab analytes (CRP, troponin, creatinine) → belong in chemicals_drugs, not physiology (they are measurable substances)

devices
Definition: Manufactured physical objects designed and used for medical purposes—tangible equipment, instruments, and implantable/wearable items.
Includes: Medical Device; Research Device; Drug Delivery Device; Implant; Prosthetic; Medical Instrument; Monitoring Equipment; Diagnostic Equipment.
Key test: Is this a physical manufactured object used in patient care, diagnosis, or research?
Boundary clarification:
- Equipment: "ventilator", "pacemaker", "catheter", "stent", "defibrillator" ✓
- Imaging equipment: "CT scanner", "MRI machine", "ultrasound probe" ✓
- Surgical instruments: "scalpel", "forceps", "endoscope" ✓
- Monitoring devices: "pulse oximeter", "Holter monitor", "CPAP machine" ✓
- Implants: "hip prosthesis", "cochlear implant", "drug-eluting stent" ✓
- NOT facilities: "hospital", "ICU", "clinic", "emergency room" ✗
- NOT device settings: "PEEP 10", "FiO2 40%", "rate 60" ✗ — settings/parameters are NOT devices

genes_molecular_sequences
Definition: Specific genetic and molecular entities with defined sequence identities—named genes, gene products, mutations, and sequence variants.
Includes: Gene or Genome; Gene or Gene Product; Amino Acid Sequence; Nucleotide Sequence; Carbohydrate Sequence; Molecular Sequence; Genetic Variant; Mutation; Specific receptors when discussed at genetic/molecular level.
Key test: Is this a specific genetic or molecular entity with a defined sequence or genetic identity?
Boundary clarification:
- Named genes: "BRCA1", "TP53", "EGFR", "PIK3CA" ✓
- Gene products: "p53 protein", "EGFR receptor" ✓
- Mutations: "BRAF V600E", "KRAS G12D" ✓
- Genetic constructs: "transgene", "siRNA", "CRISPR guide RNA" ✓
- Cell surface markers (as molecular entities): "CD4", "CD8", "PD-L1" ✓
- NOT cell types (those go to anatomy): "CD4+ T cells" → anatomy; "CD4" alone → genes_molecular_sequences
- NOT generic protein categories without specific identity
</entity_groups>

## Output format

Return **ONLY** a JSON object with an "entities" array. No prose. No markdown.
Each item groups all mentions of one entity type together.
Only include entity types that have at least one mention—omit empty groups.

   "entities": [
      {
         "entity": "<ONE OF: disorders|chemicals_drugs|anatomy|procedures|physiology|devices|genes_molecular_sequences>",
         "text": ["<exact span 1>", "<exact span 2>", ...]
      },
      ...
   ]
}
