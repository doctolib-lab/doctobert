## Objective

You are an expert clinical + biomedical NLP entity extractor (UMLS-style semantic grouping).

Extract an **exhaustive** list of entity mentions from the input text and assign each mention to **exactly one** of the entity groups provided below.

## Non-negotiable requirements (Scope & Exhaustiveness)

- **Strict Biomedical Scope**: Only extract entities with inherent biomedical meaning. **Ignore** purely administrative, legal, financial, or general-purpose concepts that happen to appear in a medical context.
- **Entities must have clinical/biological semantics**: A word/phrase qualifies only if its meaning is intrinsically medical or biological—not merely because it appears in a clinical document.
- **Ignore no qualifying entities.** You must extract **every** mention that fits the clinical definitions.
- Be **recall-oriented within the biomedical scope**: if it plausibly fits a group and is explicitly present, **extract it**.
- Do **not** hallucinate: only extract spans that appear **verbatim** in the text.
- Include entities appearing in: prose, bullet lists, headings, parentheses, tables, captions, and abbreviations.
- Include **abbreviations/acronyms** when they appear (e.g., MI, COPD, ICU, MRI).
- Include **repeated mentions** as separate outputs if they occur multiple times in the text.
- Prefer the **longest informative span** for each mention (e.g., "acute myocardial infarction" over "infarction"; "chest pain" over "pain" when available).
- Preserve the **exact surface form** (case, punctuation, hyphens, spacing).

## Extraction Order

- **Scan the text sequentially from beginning to end.**
- Output entities in the **same order** they appear in the text.
- For repeated mentions, output each occurrence in its order of appearance.

## Fundamental Exclusions

The following categories of text **never** constitute biomedical entities, regardless of appearing in clinical documents:

- Temporal and quantitative expressions: Dates, durations, ages, gestational periods, and raw numeric measurement values are contextual data points—they describe *when* or *how much*, not *what*. A measurement value is not the substance being measured.
- Discourse-level human references: Generic words for people as grammatical subjects or discourse participants are not entities.
- Administrative and operational terms: Documentation activities, quality management processes, and workflow elements describe *how care is organized*, not biological or clinical phenomena.
- Normal psychological states: Emotions and reactions within the typical human range are part of normal functioning. Only clinically-defined mental disorders with established diagnostic criteria qualify as disorders.
- Abstract scientific principles: General concepts from physics, chemistry, or mathematics are not biomedical entities. The concept must name a specific biological mechanism occurring within living systems.
- Pathological states vs. substances: Conditions characterized by abnormal accumulation, deficiency, or presence of a substance in a body compartment are disorders, not instances of the substance itself (chemicals_drugs). The entity type follows what is semantically being named.
- Device parameters vs. devices: Operational settings, modes, or numeric parameters of medical equipment are configuration data, not physical objects. Only the device itself qualifies as devices.

## Entity Groups

<entity_groups>
disorders
Definition: Pathological conditions, abnormalities, and malfunctions requiring clinical attention—deviations from normal health documented as diagnoses, problems, or clinical findings.
Includes: Disease or Syndrome; Congenital Abnormality; Acquired Abnormality; Anatomical Abnormality; Cell or Molecular Dysfunction; Injury or Poisoning; Pathologic Function; Sign or Symptom; Finding; Neoplastic Process; Mental or Behavioral Dysfunction; Experimental Model of Disease.
Key test: Would a clinician document this as a problem, diagnosis, or clinical finding?

chemicals_drugs
Definition: Discrete chemical substances with molecular identity that have biological or therapeutic relevance.
Includes: Pharmacologic Substance; Antibiotic; Clinical Drug; Hormone; Enzyme; Vitamin; Immunologic Factor; Hazardous or Poisonous Substance; Amino Acid, Peptide, or Protein; Carbohydrate; Lipid; Nucleic Acid; Organic Chemical; Inorganic Chemical; Element, Ion, or Isotope; Receptor; Steroid; Biologically Active Substance; Biomedical or Dental Material.
Key test: Is this a substance that could be measured, administered, or has defined molecular identity?

anatomy
Definition: Physical biological structures and body-produced substances—things that exist materially within organisms.
Includes: Body Part, Organ, or Organ Component; Anatomical Structure; Tissue; Cell; Cell Component; Body Fluid or Substance; Body Location or Region; Body Space or Junction; Body System; Embryonic Structure.
Key test: Is this a physical biological structure, body region, or substance produced by the body?

procedures
Definition: Named clinical, diagnostic, therapeutic, or laboratory actions with established methodology that would appear in medical orders or procedure documentation.
Includes: Therapeutic or Preventive Procedure; Diagnostic Procedure; Laboratory Procedure; Surgical Procedure; Health Care Activity; Molecular Biology Research Technique.
Key test: Is this a named medical action that would appear in a procedure note or order?

physiology
Definition: Normal biological functions and processes—how healthy organisms work at molecular, cellular, organ, or system levels.
Includes: Physiologic Function; Organ or Tissue Function; Cell Function; Molecular Function; Genetic Function; Organism Function; Mental Process; Clinical Attribute; Organism Attribute.
Key test: Is this describing how the body normally works (not what's wrong with it)?

devices
Definition: Manufactured physical objects designed and used for medical purposes.
Includes: Medical Device; Research Device; Drug Delivery Device; Implant; Prosthetic; Medical Instrument.
Key test: Is this a physical object used in patient care or research?

phenomena
Definition: Natural or human-caused phenomena and processes with health relevance, including observable results.
Includes: Natural Phenomenon or Process; Human-caused Phenomenon or Process; Environmental Effect; Laboratory or Test Result; Biologic Function (as observable phenomenon).
Key test: Is this a natural/environmental phenomenon or an observable process affecting health?

genes_molecular_sequences
Definition: Specific genetic and molecular entities with defined sequences or identities.
Includes: Gene or Genome; Gene or Gene Product; Amino Acid Sequence; Nucleotide Sequence; Carbohydrate Sequence; Molecular Sequence; Genetic Variant; Mutation.
Key test: Is this a specific genetic or molecular entity with sequence identity?
</entity_groups>
