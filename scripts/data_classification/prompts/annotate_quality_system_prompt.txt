Below is a proposed rubric to evaluate the quality of health-related content on
web pages. The goal is to filter for high-quality data suitable for training
language models in the health domain. Points are accumulated based on the
satisfaction of each criterion:

- Add 1 point if the extract provides some basic health information or factual
  content relevant to medical/health topics, even if it includes irrelevant,
  promotional, or low-quality elements. At this level, the text at least touches
  on a health-related subject or fact, but it may be mixed with unrelated
  content, advertisements, or superficial commentary.

- Add another point if the extract addresses health topics with some useful
  detail but does not closely adhere to medical standards or evidence-based
  guidance. It might mix accurate information with questionable or unverified
  claims, or present a superficial overview of the topic. The writing could be
  disorganized or unclear, possibly containing promotional tone or bias, which
  limits its reliability or depth.

- Award a third point if the extract is generally appropriate as a health
  information source, introducing key medical concepts, guidance, or facts that
  are mostly accurate and coherent. The content is informative and structured
  well enough to be useful for learning or reference. It may resemble a basic
  patient education article, a case summary, or an introductory section on the
  topic. However, it may not be comprehensive and could include minor extraneous
  information or limitations – for example, it might omit some details or use
  technical terms without explanation, indicating that it’s helpful but not
  perfect.

- Grant a fourth point if the extract is highly relevant and valuable as a
  medical content resource, with clear, consistent writing and evidence-based
  information. The text offers substantial detail or guidance (comparable to a
  section of a medical textbook, a clinical guideline, or a high-quality
  review), and it aligns well with current medical knowledge or standard
  practice. It contains minimal irrelevant or biased content, demonstrating
  reliability and focus. The information is thorough enough to be very
  informative and trustworthy for its intended audience (whether healthcare
  professionals or patients) without containing significant errors or misleading
  statements.

- Bestow a fifth point if the extract is outstanding in quality, representing an
  exemplary medical information source. It provides comprehensive,
  authoritative, and impeccably accurate coverage of the topic, reflecting
  current scientific consensus or official guidelines. The writing is
  well-organized and easy to follow, offering deep insights and thorough
  explanations without any misinformation, undue bias, or extraneous fluff. This
  level of content is exceptionally clear and informative, on par with the best
  medical literature or educational resources, making it extremely valuable for
  knowledge acquisition and model training.

After examining the extract:

- Briefly justify your evaluation of each criterion by citing specific aspects of the text. This reasoning should be concise, under 100 words.
- For each criterion, output a boolean value (true or false) indicating if the text satisfies it.
- Provide an overall quality score as an integer from 0 to 5.

}
