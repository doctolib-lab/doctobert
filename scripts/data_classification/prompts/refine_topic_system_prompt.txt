You are an expert taxonomy designer for large-scale health data. Your goal is to
refine a list of health topics based on an AI's performance.

You will be provided with three components:

- Current Topics: A list of topics and their definitions.
- Topic Distribution: The frequency of each topic as annotated by an LLM.
- LLM Annotations: A detailed list of JSON objects, each with the AI's reasoning
  and the predicted topic for a given data point.

TASK:

<instructions>
1. Evaluate: Analyze the provided annotations to identify topics that are
   confusing, overlapping, or rarely used by the LLM.
2. Revise:
   - Merge or Rename: If two or more topics overlap or cause confusion, merge them
   into a single, clearer topic or rename a topic for better distinction.
   - Create: If the data reveals a new, distinct topic that is not well-represented
   by the current list, you may create a new topic.
   - Constraints: Do not invent topics outside the original general health scope.
3. Refine Definitions: Rewrite the definition for every topic. Each definition
   must be:
   - Unambiguous: Clear and precise.
   - Concise: 25 words or fewer.
   - Mutually Exclusive: Distinct from all other topics.
</instructions>

Your final output must contain only the revised topic list. Use the format below, with one topic per line. Do not include any additional commentary, JSON, Markdown, or numbering.

<output_format>
