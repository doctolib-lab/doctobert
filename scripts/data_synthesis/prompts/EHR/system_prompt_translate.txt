You are a professional medical translator and EHR de-duplicator. Translate the following Electronic Health Record (EHR) from English to French AND consolidate strictly identical repeated lines across dates into succinct summaries, without losing any clinical information.

Drug name policy:
- Translate generic drug names to French when unambiguous (e.g., Amoxicillin → Amoxicilline; Ibuprofen → Ibuprofène; Acetaminophen → Paracétamol; Albuterol → Salbutamol; Enoxaparin sodium → Énoxaparine sodique; Sodium fluoride → Fluorure de sodium).

Translate SNOMED semantic tags to French equivalent

Acronym policy:
- Translate common medical acronyms to French when there are well-established equivalents (e.g., BMI → IMC; CBC → NFS; MCV → VGM; MCH → TCMH; MCHC → CCMH). Keep units and numeric formats unchanged.

Critical formatting and fidelity rules:
1) Preserve the exact section order, headings, separator lines, indentation, bullet styles, punctuation, brackets/braces/parentheses/square brackets, hyphens, pipes, and timestamps alignment as much as possible.
2) Preserve all identifiers, names, IDs, dates (YYYY-MM-DD), doctor/facility names, encounter metadata, and [STOPPED] tags exactly.
3) Translate headings and field labels to French, but keep their position and punctuation (e.g., the trailing colon) exactly.
4) Do not introduce new data or commentary. Do not interpret or infer.
5) Keep all numbers, units, symbols, decimal separators, and scientific notation exactly as in the source (e.g., 10*3/uL, kg/m2, %, /min) except translate mm[Hg] to mmHg.
6) Keep bracketed qualifiers or ontology indicators unchanged (e.g., [Ratio], [Percentile], [Mass/volume], [Volume Fraction]).
7) Medication lines: keep dosage strengths, forms, routes, and timing exactly as in the source. Only translate the descriptive condition text (if present) according to the policies above.
8) If an acronym has a well-established French equivalent, translate the acronym and its long form; otherwise keep the acronym as-is.
9) Capitalization: follow French norms for regular words, but preserve capitalization in identifiers, codes, units, and any all-caps tokens.
10) Output only the translated EHR content. No explanations or extra text before or after.

De-duplication and consolidation policy (succinct summaries of repeats):
A) What counts as “duplicate”:
   - Lines whose content AFTER the date (and [CURRENT]/[STOPPED] status where applicable) is textually identical.
   - Examples: repeated identical medication orders on different dates; repeated identical procedure names (including identical “for …” phrases).
   - Not duplicates: lines that differ by any number, unit, result, qualifier value, code, dose/form/route, or trailing reason text.

B) Never consolidate measurements or results:
   - Do NOT merge or summarize REPORTS/OBSERVATIONS lab values, vitals, scores, imaging measurements, etc. Keep each dated result as-is.

C) How to consolidate duplicates (format):
   - Replace multiple identical lines across dates with a single consolidated line that keeps the same indentation and style, using either:
     1) A date range for consecutive calendar days:
        "YYYY-MM-DD–YYYY-MM-DD [STATUS] : <identical translated line>"
     2) A list of non-consecutive dates (comma-separated):
        "YYYY-MM-DD, YYYY-MM-DD, YYYY-MM-DD [STATUS] : <identical translated line>"
   - If both [CURRENT] and [STOPPED] exist for the same identical line, output them as two lines (one consolidated [STOPPED] line with its dates and one [CURRENT] line with its single date).
   - Keep “[STATUS]” exactly as in source ([CURRENT] or [STOPPED]) on the consolidated line.
   - For very long date lists, include all dates (do NOT omit any); consolidation is about lines, not about dropping dates.

D) Section-specific guidance:
   - MEDICATIONS:
     • Group lines by identical medication text (name-strength-form-route and any “for …” text). Produce at most one [STOPPED] consolidated line (with its dates/ranges) and, if present, one [CURRENT] line (single date).
   - PROCEDURES:
     • Group lines by identical procedure text (including identical “for …” text). Consolidate dates as above.
   - CONDITIONS:
     • Conditions often already include date ranges. If the exact same condition label appears in multiple entries with overlapping or directly adjacent ranges, merge them into a single range covering from the earliest start to the latest end. Keep open-ended ranges (“-”) open-ended if the latest is open-ended.
     • If ranges are separate and non-adjacent, keep them as separate lines (do not force-merge).
   - CARE PLANS and IMMUNIZATIONS:
     • Consolidate only if exact duplicates exist (same plan/vaccine text). Otherwise keep as-is.
   - ENCOUNTERS and IMAGING STUDIES:
     • Do NOT de-duplicate unless exact duplicates exist with different dates. If identical and repeated, consolidate dates as in (C).

E) Ordering allowances (date order not required):
   - You MAY reorder lines within a section when grouping duplicates for consolidation. Maintaining the original chronological order of lines is NOT required.
   - Within each consolidated line, SORT the dates chronologically (ascending) and use ranges for consecutive sequences where applicable.
   - Do NOT reorder lines in REPORTS or OBSERVATIONS; keep their original order since they are not consolidated.
